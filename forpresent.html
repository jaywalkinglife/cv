Cooking after a long day of work or college seems like an insane chore. However, as with most things, when you are looking at an issue in a microscopic level, everything seems cumbersome. Instead look at it this way, you are arranging a set amount of ingredients on a heat source and when you are done, it will fill you with sweet caloric joy. And atleast, you didn't have to go out and hunt or gather your food today

General Tips

  1. Clean everything up as you cook, you have the momentum, use it! - p.s Dishwashers are for the weak
  2. Prep ahead, if you have everything (ingredients) ready to be sacrificed to the flames, the cooking will be a breeze
  3. Listen to some music, I know everyone says, "Trust the process", I say "Enjoy the process"

  • Marinated Tofu with eggs

    This meal is packed with nutrients including protein and a good amounts of healthy fat! It's vegetarian friendly, keto friendly and it tastes delicious

    What you need:

    1. 6.75 ounces of Extra Firm Tofu (can substitute this with chicken for better protein and less fat content)
    2. 6 ounces of mushrooms (or your favorite types of veggies like onions, tomatoes, potatoes, etc.)
    3. 2 slices of muenster cheese (feel free to substitute with your favorite cheese)
    4. 4 large eggs
    5. 1 regular sized frying pan
    6. 2 tbsp coconut oil (feel free to substitute with butter or Olive oil)
    7. 1.5 tbsp garlic marinade (feel free to substitute with your favorite marinade)
    8. Chipotle Flavored Tabasco Sauce ot taste (optional)

    What to do:

    • Step 0.1: Remove Tofu from the packaged container, and cut them into small pieces as shown and then dump that into the ziplock bag with your desired marinade and add some water and wait a minimum of 2 hours or leave it overnight (that package will typically make 2 servings based on this recipe!)
    • Step 1: Heat the pan to medium heat and add oil after about 20 seconds
    • Step 2: Add the marinated tofu or chicken to pan, cook for about 10-15 minutes. Stir as you go.
    • Step 3: Lower the heat a bit and while the tofu cooks, wash the mushrooms under cold water and add them to the pan
    • Step 4: Cook for another 9-11 minutes, its your choice if you want to leave a bit of the gravy or have it completely dry
    • Step 5: Remove the cooked tofu and mushrooms and set them aside
    • Step 6: Add 4 whisked eggs into the pan (the eggs will soak up the remaining oil and spices)
    • Step 7: Heat for around 4-7 minutes under medium heat (feel free to add your favorite spice on the eggs)
    • Step 8: Flip over the omelette and add two slices of cheese
    • Step 9: Wait around 2-3 minutes and then fold the omelette in half so that it sandwiches the two cheese slices
    • Step 10: Place the semi-circled omelette on the plate like shown in the picture and you are good to go!
    • Step 11: Wash your dishes and tackle the rest of the day!

    What you need:

    1. A 6.3 ounce raw Tuna Steak
    2. Garlic & Herbs seasoning (or your favorite choice of seasoning and spices)
    3. 1 tbsp of olive oil
    4. 6 ounces of broccoli (handful)
    5. A frying pan

    What to do:

    • Step 1: Remove the prepackeged tuna steak from its plastic packaging
    • Step 2: Dry the steak with paper towels and sprinkle your favorite seasoning (you can also marinade this a couple hours earlier for a different flavor)
    • Step 3: Heat the pan on medium for about 20-30 seconds, add 1 tbsp of olive oil
    • Step 4: Spread the oil around the pan and add the tuna steak
    • Step 5: Flip the steak so its cooked from both sides, do around 3-4 minutes per side on medium heat or until the pink disappears
    • Step 6: After the steak is cooked, place it aside. Now you have two options for the broccoli: 1. Add it to the pan with some water and cook for about 2 minutes. Or use a steamer and steam the broccoli (microwave it in a bowl with a wet papertowel on it)
    • Step 7: Eat this simple but nutritious meal, wash the dishes and feel like a god walking among mere mortals!
